About Me

I’ve been riding for the last 18 years. It didn’t start with anything big—just a simple 100cc bike, the kind most people begin with. Back then, it was just about getting from one place to another. But somewhere along the way, the ride became something more.

What keeps me riding isn’t speed or distance—it’s the experience. The feeling of being out there, away from everything, surrounded by nature. The sound of the engine fades into the background, and what remains is the road, the wind, and a quiet sense of awareness. In those moments, I try to understand where I stand in this vast world.

Every ride becomes a story. Not just about the places I visit, but about what I feel along the way. The changing landscapes, the silence of the mountains, the unpredictability of the journey—they all leave something behind. I try to capture those moments, not just through pictures or videos, but through the way I experience them.

Motorcycling, for me, is more than a hobby. It’s a feeling—something difficult to explain, but deeply real. It’s freedom, reflection, and connection, all at once. And no matter how many years pass, that feeling never fades.
